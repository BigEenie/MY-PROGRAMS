About Pastor Adenekan

Licensed Marriage & Family Therapist PhD in Clinical Psychology 15+ Years Experience

With over 15 years of experience in relationship counseling, I specialize in helping couples reconnect and strengthen their bonds. My approach combines evidence-based therapeutic techniques with compassionate understanding to create a safe space for healing and growth.

I believe that every relationship has the potential for positive change. My philosophy centers on empowering couples with the tools and insights needed to communicate effectively, resolve conflicts constructively, and build deeper intimacy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our counseling services

How long is each counseling session?

+

Individual therapy sessions are typically 50 minutes, while couples and family sessions are 60-90 minutes. This allows enough time to explore issues thoroughly and make meaningful progress.

Do you offer virtual sessions?

+

Yes, we offer secure video sessions for clients who prefer the convenience of virtual therapy or cannot attend in-person sessions. Virtual sessions are just as effective as in-person sessions for most clients.

How many sessions will we need?

+

The number of sessions varies depending on your specific goals and circumstances. Some couples see improvement in 6-12 sessions, while others may benefit from longer-term therapy. We'll discuss your progress regularly and adjust as needed.

Is everything we discuss confidential?

+

Yes, everything discussed in therapy is confidential and protected by law. There are only very limited exceptions, such as if there's an immediate risk of harm to yourself or others. We'll discuss confidentiality in detail during your first session.

Do you accept insurance?

+

We accept most major insurance plans and can provide you with receipts for reimbursement. We recommend checking with your insurance provider about your mental health benefits and coverage for couples therapy.

What if only one partner wants to attend?

+

While couples therapy works best when both partners participate, individual therapy can still be very beneficial. Often, changes made by one partner can positively impact the relationship and sometimes encourage the other partner to join later.
